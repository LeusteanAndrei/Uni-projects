 Tengen Toppa Gurren Lagann, commonly reffered to as Gurren Lagann, is a japanese mecha anime animated by Gainax Studios. It's got 27 episode and aired from the 1st April 2007 to the 30th of September 2007. Since it's release, the anime and those involved in producing it have received several awards. The series received and Excellency Prize at the 2007 Japan Media Arts Festival, the director Hiroyuki Imaishi received an individual award for "Personal Best" at the 12th Animation Kobe Festival. During the 7th annual Tokyo Anime Awards it also received the "Best Television Series" award and the "Best Character Design" award, given to the character designer Atsushi Nishigori.

 Ever since first appearing on television, Gurren Lagann has been a influential anime foar all those to come. It's managed to captivate the hearth of both those searching for something fun or something profound. Hiding behind the superficial and over the top presentation lies a meaningful message worth hearing. Bearing many of the beloved anime tropes Gurren Lagann is a mecha like no other, with its over the top designs and larger than life characters, the show doesn't hide from all these cliches many are bored of, it instead embraces them.

The story takes place in a distopian future, a time where humans have been forced to live underground by some unknown reasons. We follow Simon and Kamina, two people born and raised underground, never having seen the sky. One day, while Simon was digging tunnels to expand the city, he find a giant face staring at them which Kamina calls Lagann. Soon after, they are attacked by a mysterious mecha and, after defeating it with the help of Yoko Littner and their newfound weapon, the two climb up to the surface. While airborn above the desolate wasteland they see the sky for the first time. From here on out starts their journey in unveiling the cosmic mysteries of their world.

Gurren Lagann has received widespread critical acclaim ever since its release. With great reviews all over the internet and high ratings, it has easily become one of the most popular mecha anime and an inspiration for many more shows to come. These influences can be found all the way from Japanese anime and games, to comics and other animated tv shows and even in politics, more precisely in England.

During a political debate over wether the British Union Flag should cotain the Welsh Dragon, The Daily Telegraph held a contest for readers to submit their designs and have other readers vote for the best one. On December 11 2007, a Gurren Lagann based design submitted from Norway, won the contest with a wide margin of 55% of votes.

The most important part of Gurren Lagann is definitiley its style in music, animation and design. Everything is very striking, from the looks of the robots to the signature song "Libera me from hell". A very important element of the designs are the glasses. every one of the main trio has their pair, Kamina's being the most iconic one's.

The mecha design in Gurren Lagann is nothing short of awe-inspiring. From the initial appearance of the Lagann, a small but resilient machine, to the towering and formidable forms it evolves into, each iteration showcases a remarkable attention to detail and creativity. The robots in the series are not just machines but embodiments of sheer determination and indomitable spirit, reflecting the overarching themes of overcoming obstacles and reaching for the impossible. The fusion of mechanical elements with bold and dynamic aesthetics creates a visual spectacle that perfectly complements the series' epic scale and intense action sequences. Whether it's the iconic face of the Gurren Lagann or the imposing presence of the larger mechs, the robot designs in Gurren Lagann leave an indelible mark on the viewer, embodying the very essence of the show's boundless ambition and unwavering resolve.

The Gurren Lagann robot stands as more than just a formidable weapon within the series. From its humble beginnings to its ultimate evolution as the simbol of hope and defiance against oppression, the Gurren Lagann symbolizes the journey of its pilots and their unwavering resolve to break free from the shackles of fate.

Lazengann, the mecha belonging to Lordgenome, stands as a stark contrast to the vibrant Gunmen of Team Gurren. Its design reflects Lordgenome's character both through shape and colour. While Team Gurren's gunmen look like robots built in a scrapeyard, Lazengann is meticulosuly crafted, exuding an aura of menace and power.
