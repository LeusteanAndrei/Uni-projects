The start of Gurren Lagann can be tracked down all the way back to the partnership between Gainax and Aniplex, two of the most influential anime figures of that time. Through the people working on it bringin all sort of innovative ideas, the show turned out to be a love letter to the mecha anime genre, while also creating its own identity. Gainax studios came to be in the 1980's when a group of college students which loved animation and robots made their first succesful animated project, a short film paying omage to the classic mecha anime of that time, which was presented at the 20th Annual Japan National SF Convention, called Daicon IV. First called Daicon Film, the small studio changed its name to Gainax in 1986, the name is formed from an Tottori Prefecture term for the word giant and the suffix 'x' because "it sounded cool".

It took some time for the studio to really come into its own but it gained its footing with the huge release of Neon Genesis Evangelion in 1995, written and directed by Hideaki Anno. In a very short period of time Evangelion achieved international recognition, captivating the audience with its complex story and philosophical themes. Following the succes of Evangelion, and the questions posed by the show, Hiroyuki Imaishi, (who worked as a lead animator on Eva) started to envision a story that would capture the spirit of the mecha genre, similar in a way to Evangelion but in a totally different way.

Evangelion was not just a succesful anime but also a milestone, laying the groundwork for many of the popular tropes to come, inspiring a great number of anime to have deeper meanings. A lot of the staff that would later work on Gurren Lagann, also worked on Evangelion, and this aspect can be seen becaues the two shows are, in a way, two sides of the same coin. Altough radically different in execution, behind all the story and characters, Gurren Lagann and Evangelion both approach the same themes: individuality, consciousness, freedom of choice, responsibility and the search of one's own identity.

Over the years, Gainax has continued to produce many hit anime shows such as Fooly Cooly, Panty and Stocking and many more after it became Studio Trigger( Kill la Kill, Cyberpunk ...). Through all of these shows, a clear attention to style can be remarked, each and everyone of them having their own appeal not just in animation and character design but also in presentation of its story and themes. This is predominantly visible in Gurren Lagann in which every single aspect is approached with the same philosphy at its core: "If you are doing it, I'm doing it. If I don't do it, will you? If you don't do it, I'll do it. I'm doing it so that you'll do it too!!" (Kamina).

The story

Gurren Lagann takes place in a world where humans are trapped beneath the surface, where humanity lives its life in isolated underground villages. While digging tunnels to greaten his village, Simon finds a big face and shows it to his "blood brother" Kamina. Instantly Simon is portrayed as shy, lacking self-confidence, in contrast to Kamina, confident and charistmatic young man, determined to overthrow the status quo.

In a weird turn of fate, the a giant robot falls upon them destroying the ceiling. Simon and the rest of the village are scared, but Kamina is finally excited, this is the moment he has been waiting for. Taking his sword in hand and announcing his name, he confidently faces the giant behemoth in front of him. In that moment Yoko comes in, a person from the surface, with a huge gun. The three of them, Simon, Kamina and Yoko manage to defeath the giant robot, with the power of the face found by Simon, and climb up to the surface. There Kamina and Simon witness inimaginable beauty as they see the sky for the first time.

Having reached the surface, the three learn that the ones oppresing humans are beastmen controlling giant robots. The first idea that comes to Kamina's mind is to steal the robot and fight back. Altough everyone thinks it's stupid, he pushes on with his idea and, with the help of Simon's Lagann, he manages to steal a robot, naming it Gurren. This brazen atitude of Kamina is a trademark of his character. Later when fighting Viral in his Enkid, a mecha with two faces, Kamina gets jealous and wishes he had two face. This is when he proposes the preposterous ideea to combine with Simon and takes Lagann and shoves it in Gurren's head. Seemingly out of nowhere the two robots start combining, forming the newly improved Gurren Lagann.

  • While most mecha anime openings of that time started with a shot illustratin the robot or the protagonist(usually watching the sky), Gurren Lagann's Sorairo Days starts of with the charatcers waking up, which further suggests that the show is an affectionate parody of the mecha genre.
  • In classic anime style every single attack is screamed. Many of these attacks have comically long names: "Who the hel do you think i am punch!!!".
  • Gurren Lagann was released first as an anime but it has gained a number of extra animated shorts and side episodes with the releas of the dvd. This also includes the 15 "Parallel works" music videos, a video manga called Viral's Sweet Dream and a director's cut of episode 6.
  • Ther is also a manga based on the anime which contains some extra content such as Kiyal's comedic rebellion and Yoko attaining her Gunmen.
  • Lord Genome's four generals are named after the four nucleotide bases found in DNA: Cytosine (Cytomander), Guanine (Guame), Adenine (Adiane), and Thymine (Thymilph).
  • Every episode's title is a line said by a character during the respective episode.
  • Gurren Laggan was greatly infleunced by the Getter Robo manga, by Ken Ishikawa.
  • About 40% of the show's budget was reserved for the last two episodes to not have the same problems the had with Evangelion.
  • The very first scene was supposed to be a part of ending but was lately scrapped.
  • The shows theme song Libera me from Hell mashes two radically different genres together, opera and rap. The opera part is sung in latin and describes the coming of the end of the world, representing the oppresive force. The rap part represents a rebellious attitude much like team Dai Gurren's. The song played throughout episodes 23 and 27 illustrates team Dai Gurren's fight against the anti-spirals.
